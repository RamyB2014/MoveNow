Enter GCI Depth

To enter the GCI (gross completion interval) depth, go to Economics | Interests & Royalties.

For wells in Alberta, the GCI (gross completion interval) is used to calculate the depth factor (DF). A depth factor is calculated based on the measured depth (MD), according to the records of the ERCB for that well event.

For a well event, the MD is defined as the longest distance, in meters, along the bore of the well (horizontally and/or vertically) from the Kelly bushing of the well event to the bottom of the gross completion interval (GCI) from which the well event is producing.

The DF is used in the determination of the quantity component (rq) of the royalty rate, for measured depths (MD) that exceed 2000 meters.

The DF for a well event is determined based on the following formula:

DF = (MD / 2000) 2

If MD <= 2,000m, then DF = 1.0 (minimum DF)

If MD > 2,000m and < 4,000m, then DF = (MD / 2000) 2

If MD >= 4,000m, then DF = 4.0 (maximum DF)

The GCI depth can be loaded with production data.

To enter Measured Depth or True Vertical Depth, go to Economics | Interests & Royalties.
