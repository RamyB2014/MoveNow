Theoretical Yield

Theoretical Yield is displayed on Predictions | Data | Plant. It is based on the Inlet Gas Analysis and is a plant-based value. There is no Theoretical Yield in Sales mode because plant efficiencies are not valid in this mode.

Theoretical Yield is expressed in terms of the gas stream you are entering ratios on.

The theoretical yield is the amount of product that would be recovered assuming a recovery factor of 100%. uses the following product factors when calculating Theoretical Yield:

Item Product Factor
Ethane (C2) 6.35
Propane (C3) 6.54
Butane (C4) 7.62
Pentane (C5+) 9.6

The product factors are based on industry standards. The formula for Theoretical Yields is as follows:

Raw Yield = %Vol * Product Factor * (1-Surface Loss)
(Note that Yield is a plant-based value so yield expressed as raw gas needs to consider surface loss) Inlet Yield = %Vol * Product Factor Example Volume % for C2 = 7.27 (obtained from Master Gas Analysis) Product Factor for C2 = 6.35 Theoretical Yield = 7.27 x 6.35 = 46.1645 bbl/MMcf
