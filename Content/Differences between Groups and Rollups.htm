Differences between Groups and Rollups

Groups are useful for evaluating commingled wells, or when you want to group wells in a unit. Groups are not ideal for entities that have incentives or quantity-based royalties, because the averaging effect could lead to inaccurate results. Rollups are useful for tracking drilling programs. The differences between groups and rollups are described below.

Groups Rollups
Groups are entities. Rollups are lists of wells.
Wells in a group appear in the well list as a subset of their group parent. Wells in a rollup still appear in the well list.
You can run economics on a group, and you can see economic results of the child wells, though they are overridden at the group level. You cannot run economics on a rollup.
The group gets a 100% WI, and the average on-time is calculated based on the total producing hours / total calendar hours for each step. The rollup on-time is calculated for each forecast month and is the Rollup month’s production volume / (Rollup daily production rate * Days in month).
