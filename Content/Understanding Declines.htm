Understanding Declines

allows you to add segments (using the Calculator) to your declines as new data parameters are introduced. You can work with three types of declines: hyperbolic, exponential, and harmonic. You can also work with six types of forecasts: oil, gas, water, 1+WOR (Water Oil Ratio), GOR (Gas Oil Ratio), WGR (Water Gas Ratio).

The following values are required to calculate a decline:

Decline Parameter Description
Qi The flow rate of the forecasted fluid at the start date (initial rate)
Qf The final or minimum rate
Di (nom) or Di (eff. sec) or Di (eff. tan) The initial Nominal, Effective Secant, or Effective Tangent decline rate (see Forecasting Declines: Effective and Nominal Rates
Df (nom) or Df (eff. sec) or Df (eff. tan) The final Nominal, Effective Secant or Effective Tangent decline rate (see Forecasting Declines: Effective and Nominal Rates
Ni (b) The hyperbolic factor.  Ni (b) = 0 is exponential; Ni (b) = 1 is harmonic; Ni (b) <> 0,1 is hyperbolic
Max Rate The maximum liquid production rate - oil plus water

For more information, see Calculate Decline Parameters.
