Enter Capital Costs

Enter capital costs on Economics | Prices & Costs | Capital Costs. When you enter capital costs, enter the gross amount. The working interest share is calculated according to the percentage entered for Capital Cost Interest on Economics | Interests & Royalties. See Enter a Working Interest.

Inflation is applied to each cost according to the capital cost inflation rate entered in the Price Deck Editor. The default inflation rate is zero. See Edit Inflation.

By default, all capital costs after the economic calculation date are included in the payout reversion calculation. However, you can exclude a capital cost from the payout reversion calculation (or apply a multiple) using the Reversion Factor. See the procedure below.

When entering abandonment or salvage costs on a well with no forecast, you must enter the costs as an override in any reserves category other than Common. When abandonment and salvage are entered in Common on a well with no forecast, the costs are not calculated and do not appear in reports.

You can enter capital costs in three categories: Common, Success and Failure. Under these categories, you can select Working Interest or Facility Interest costs. Under these categories, costs are organized by type.

  • Common costs: incurred regardless of success or failure.
  • Success costs: incurred only if the project is successful.
  • Failure costs: incurred only if the project fails.
  • Working Interest costs: costs that a company has an interest in.
  • Facility Interest costs: costs for a facility that a company has an interest in.

To enter capital costs

  1. Go to Economics | Prices & Costs | Capital Costs and click Add Costs.
  2. Move costs from the Available Costs window to the Selected Costs window by double-clicking them.
  3. Click OK.
  4. At the top of the cost column, select a Cost Stream (common, success, or failure).
  5. If you want to prevent inflation, select Prevent Inflation.
  6. If you want to exclude the cost from the payout reversion calculation, change the Reversion Factor to 0.
  7. In each cost column, enter the amount of each cost next to the date on which it occurs.
  8. If a cost category name is appended by (GCA) it is used to calculate GCA allowance.

Tips

  • You can change the date display under Display Mode, in the bottom right corner of the screen.
  • You can copy costs to other wells using the Input Copier.
  • You can rename costs by typing in the Name field at the top of the column. However, this does not rename this cost for the entire project.

For the GCA classes associated with each capital cost, see Capital Cost Allowance (CCA) Classes.
